Team Roles

Every engagement moves through the pipeline carried by a defined set of roles. Each role maps to a stage and a responsibility. Here’s how to talk about each one.

👤
Admin
admin
Full system access. Manages users, configures roles, and has visibility across all engagements and schemas. This is the operator of the platform itself.
All stagesSystem config
Talking Points
  • Think of Admin as the back-office operator — they see everything but touch the client-facing flow sparingly.
  • In our model, André and the core team hold this role initially.
  • Role provisioning is enforced at the database level via RLS, not just app logic.
🔍
Sales Agent
sales_agent
Owns the top of the funnel. Handles inbound leads, qualifies prospects, opens engagements, and shepherds the client through intake and scoping.
IntakeScoping
Talking Points
  • This is the first human touchpoint after a lead submits the intake form.
  • They qualify the engagement and assign the service tier before handing off.
  • In a small team, one person can cover sales_agent and operator_agent — the role separation still matters for auditability.
📄
Operator Agent
operator_agent
Takes the baton from Sales after scoping is complete. Writes the proposal, coordinates delivery, and manages the active engagement from proposal through review.
ProposalActiveReview
Talking Points
  • The Operator is the project manager — they own execution quality and client communication during the work.
  • They’re the ones who move a stage forward in the system once milestones are hit.
  • This role is where most of the billable coordination happens.
💻
Analyst Agent
analyst_agent
The technical practitioner. Runs the actual analysis, builds data pipelines, develops dashboards, and produces the deliverables tied to the engagement.
ActiveReview
Talking Points
  • Analysts are assigned to engagements, not companies — a single analyst can work multiple active engagements.
  • Their work is tracked at the task level inside each engagement.
  • This is where the Tier system matters most — Tier 3 (Expert Analysis) always has an analyst_agent as lead.
🎓
Educator Agent
educator_agent
Leads Tier 4 data education engagements. Designs and delivers training, workshops, and enablement programs for client teams.
ScopingActive
Talking Points
  • Tier 4 is one of our highest-margin offerings — the Educator is the value driver here.
  • They co-design curriculum with the Operator during scoping, then own delivery.
  • The same role can also support analyst engagements where client upskilling is a component.
🤝
Client Agent
client_agent
The external-facing role. Clients and authorized client-side contacts have scoped, read-only visibility into their own engagement status and deliverables.
ActiveReviewComplete
Talking Points
  • Clients only see their own data — this is enforced by Supabase RLS, not application logic.
  • This creates a natural client portal with zero additional infrastructure.
  • In the future, clients could log in and check status, download deliverables, or approve proposals — all within the same system.
🔒
Viewer
viewer
Read-only access to internal pipeline data. Useful for stakeholders, advisors, or external partners who need visibility but no edit permissions.
Read-onlyAll stages
Talking Points
  • Viewer is the safe default for onboarding new team members before assigning a functional role.
  • Also useful for board-level or investor reporting — they can see pipeline health without touching anything.
  • All permission boundaries are live in the database today, not planned for later.
